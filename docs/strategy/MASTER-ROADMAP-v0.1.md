## 2. Immutable Core Principles

These principles are expected to survive all future roadmap revisions.

### 2.1. Proposal Is Not Authority

No model, agent, proposer, or runtime component may become sovereign authority. All proposals must pass governance.

### 2.2. Verification Does Not Authorize Action

Verification alone never grants execution authority. Authorization remains separate.

### 2.3. Replayability Is Foundational

A cognition system that cannot reproduce behavior cannot sustain institutional trust. Replay continuity is foundational infrastructure.

### 2.4. Refusal Is A Valid Runtime Outcome

The runtime must preserve the ability to refuse invalid, contradictory, unsafe, unverifiable, or unauthorized transitions.

### 2.5. Canonicalization Is Infrastructure

Canonical bytes define interoperability continuity. Silent canonicalization drift is unacceptable.

### 2.6. Auditability Is Mandatory

Every meaningful state transition must remain inspectable.

### 2.7. Governance Is More Important Than Fluency

The system exists to govern consequence formation, not maximize persuasive generation.

### 2.8. Operational Truth Over Hype

All claims must remain:

- measurable,
- testable,
- reproducible,
- and pressure-testable.

### 2.9. Human Approval Remains Sovereign

No autonomous authority transfer is permitted. Humans remain final authority.

### 2.10. Continuity Over Velocity

The architecture must evolve slower than hype cycles.
